**Bayer, JMM**, van Velzen, LS, Pozzi, E, Davey, C, Han, LKM, Bauduin, SEEC, Bauer, J, Benedetti, F, Berger, K, Bonnekoh, LM, others. (2025). Dissecting heterogeneity in cortical thickness abnormalities in major depressive disorder: a large-scale ENIGMA MDD normative modelling study. bioRxiv. Submitted

**Summary:** I showed that cortical thickness abnormalities in major depressive disorder are subtle, highly individualised, and often overlap with healthy controls, using large-scale normative modelling on ENIGMA data. Extreme deviations were linked to factors such as symptom severity, age of onset, and remission, highlighting the importance of individual-level neuroimaging for precision psychiatry.

Published, Elsevier

**Summary** I developed and validated normative and hierarchical Bayesian models to account for site-related variation in multi-site neuroimaging data. This work demonstrated how these models improve the accuracy and generalisability of individual-level brain predictions, enabling more reliable large-scale analyses across diverse datasets.
